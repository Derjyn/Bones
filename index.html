Bones Overview and Usage

Bones is a simple grid layout framework for developing responsive sites utilizing flexbox. Apart from the core layout classes, there are several other utility classes that aid in designing clean and snappy websites. It can be frustrating to re-invent the wheel constantly, and having a base to start with helps increase the speed of developing a site's layout and design. Many other frameworks exist but most are very bloated and difficult to customize or rip apart for one's own devious needs.

This is where Bones comes in. Currently in the alpha stages, Bones already shines at providing a solid base to get common layouts functioning. You can view an example of a common layout built using Bones here. In the future, Bones will be married with Flesh, to become a complete site-kit. If Bones were the layout side of the coin, Flesh would be the visual side. Components within the core Bones CSS will be pulled out and moved over to Flesh, such as the print styles and typography.

The "skinny" bit of Bones, is in it's simplicity and size. With comments and before any sort of compression, the layout components (the core of Bones) takes us up under 150 lines of code. As previously mentioned, other components of the CSS in Bones will be pulled out and implemented into Flesh. Finally, you can check out a visual demo of the grid system here. Onto the usage details!

Spines and Ribs

When we reference a grid system, we generally think of elements being laid out in rows and columns. In Bones, a spine would be the row, and a rib would be a column within that row. A spine will fill it's parent container in width. Bones is based on eighths; that is, a full width rib is 100%, on down to the smallest rib being one-eighth the size of it's parent spine. Below is a reference of the rib sizes with class names.

  • rib one : 12.5%
  • rib two : 25%
  • rib three : 37.5%
  • rib four : 50%
  • rib five : 62.5%
  • rib six : 75%
  • rib seven : 87.5%
  • rib full : 100%

So for example, you could create a single row, three column layout area like this: 

                        <div class="spine">
                            <div class="rib two">CONTENT</div>
                            <div class="rib four">CONTENT</div>
                            <div class="rib two">CONTENT</div>
                        </div>
Your rib sizes don't necessarily have to add up to eight either. Many grid systems require your columns to add up to their max sizing scheme, such as a twelve column based system need to be something like 4+6+2=12. These systems sometimes break, or at the least corral you into using their sizing standard. Bones is simple to change. If you wanted, you could easily modify it to be based on 10, 12, or 16 column based sizing. To assist in making ribs easy to work with, there mod classes, which are covered below.

Mods

Several "mod" classes exist in Bones, to help more finely tweak layouts. Mods differ from utilities, in that mods specifically affect ribs. Below is an overview of these mod classes.

  • l-margin : Adds a 16px margin to the left of a rib
  • r-margin : Adds a 16px margin to the right of a rib
  • t-margin : Adds a 16px margin above a rib
  • b-margin : Adds a 16px margin below a rib
  • push-one : Pushes a rib to the right with a 12.5% margin
  • push-two : Pushes a rib to the right with a 25% margin
  • push-three : Pushes a rib to the right with a 37.5% margin
  • push-four : Pushes a rib to the right with a 50% margin
  • push-five : Pushes a rib to the right with a 62.5% margin
  • push-six : Pushes a rib to the right with a 75% margin
  • push-seven : Pushes a rib to the right with a 87.5% margin

Utilities

On top of mods, there are various utility classes that assist even more in fine tuning a layout. Over time, new utility classes may be added to Bones, but for now you can take find an overview of the current ones below.

  • body spaced - Adds a 16px margin to the page
  • body ine-sixty - Sets max width of the body to 960px
  • body twelve-eighty - Sets the body to a maximum width of 1280px
  • breaker - A hard break, 100% width
  • breaker sixteen - Sets a breaker to have a height of 16px
  • breaker thirty-two - Sets a breaker's height to 32px
  • breaker sixty-four - Sets a breaker's height to 64px
  • spine center - Sets spine justify-content to center
  • spine stretch - Sets spine align-items to stretch
  • filler - 100% width with no margin or padding (helps fix some issues, but subject to removal)
  • grower - Sets a rib to grow to its spine's remaining width
